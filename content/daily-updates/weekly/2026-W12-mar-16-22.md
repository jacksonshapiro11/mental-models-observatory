The market lost three floors this week. Gold lost $5,000. The S&P made new 2026 lows. Oil broke above $110 to the upside. All three moved fast, all three grabbed headlines. But the one that matters most for the next three months isn't the most dramatic — it's the most structural.

Physical premiums stayed elevated. Central bank buying patterns — the actual structural floor — operate on weekly and monthly timescales, not daily. The Reserve Ratchet thesis was wrong about the level ($5,000 was speculative, not structural) but right about the mechanism. It will reform lower, likely in the $4,200-4,500 range. Oil breaking $110 is the one that rewires everything else. Iraq's force majeure converted the supply disruption from "voluntary with an off-ramp" to "involuntary with no resolution mechanism." Every economic model using February or early March inputs is now structurally wrong. Every inflation forecast built on $85-95 oil needs to be rebuilt for $110+. Every Fed rate path projection needs to accommodate an inflation impulse that Goldman estimated at +40-60bp to core PCE — calibrated for lower oil prices than what we're seeing now.

The framework: when three things break simultaneously, the market treats them as three separate events. But they're one event with three expressions. Oil broke $110 because the supply cascade deepened. Gold broke $5,000 because oil positions needed liquidity. Equities broke to new lows because the inflation impulse from oil makes the rate path worse. One cause, three symptoms. The symptom that's structural — oil — is the one that determines where the others go next.

The Fed's impossible position — already impossible — became genuinely unsolvable this week. Raising rates into an oil shock guarantees recession. Holding rates guarantees stagflation. Cutting rates guarantees inflation acceleration. CME FedWatch already pricing 12% probability of a rate HIKE. The 30Y approaching 5% is the market's way of saying: we don't know which bad option you'll pick, but we know they're all bad.

**Looking ahead:** If Brent sustains above $110 for two more weeks, the April inflation data will confirm what the market is just beginning to price. The repricing of rate expectations — from cuts to hikes — will accelerate. The gold floor will reform. And equities will need to find a bottom in a world where the dominant macro variable (oil) has no visible resolution mechanism. The 1973 analog says the market that thinks it understands an oil shock in Week 3 doesn't understand it at all until the data catches up in Month 3. We're in that gap right now.
