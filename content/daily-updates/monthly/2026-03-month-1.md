# ▸ THE MONTH'S SIX — What Defined This Month

### 1. The Iran War Changed Everything
February 28: the US launched strikes on Iran after nuclear talks collapsed. What followed was a 22-day escalation that transformed the global macro picture: Hormuz effectively closed, 5-6M bpd of supply disrupted, five countries drawn into direct conflict, an F-35 hit for the first time, a submarine torpedo kill (first since WWII), Kharg Island bombed, South Pars struck, four senior Iranian officials killed in 48 hours. Both sides rejected ceasefire. The war became the dominant variable for every other market.

### 2. Oil's Supply Cascade Proved the Framework
Our very first Take of the publication mapped the second-order chain: Hormuz closure → insurance cancellation → tanker stoppage → storage overflow → production cuts → force majeure. Twenty-two days later, every link in that chain has fired. The "Economic Denial Architecture" framework (insurance cancellation creates a structural floor under oil that persists months beyond any ceasefire) has been confirmed. Iraq's force majeure was the final link — involuntary supply disruption with no resolution mechanism.

### 3. The Fed's Position Went from Difficult to Impossible
The month opened with Fed Governor Waller "looking through" tariffs and debating cuts. It ended with seven FOMC members seeing zero cuts and the market pricing rate hikes. The sequence: hot PPI → FOMC hawkish shift → oil at $112 → Iraq force majeure guaranteeing inflation data deterioration through Q2. The rate path went from "when do they cut?" to "could they actually hike?" in 28 days.

### 4. Gold's Regime Change — Liquidity Source, Not Safe Haven
Gold entered the month at $5,138 — near all-time highs, a 43% gain over 12 months, powered by central bank buying and the "Reserve Ratchet" thesis. It exited at $4,583. The crash wasn't a thesis failure — it was a regime distinction. Gold became a liquidity source for margin calls on oil positions. Paper market sold; physical premiums stayed elevated. The structural thesis (CB diversification, fiscal dominance, de-dollarization) remains intact. The price level was wrong. The mechanism was right.

### 5. Crypto Built Its Foundation While Nobody Was Looking
Three events converged during maximum fear: (1) SEC/CFTC five-category token taxonomy gave 16 tokens digital commodity status under CFTC jurisdiction. (2) Staking cleared as non-securities activity. (3) GENIUS Act became operational. Combined with persistent ETF inflows and whale accumulation during 46 days of extreme fear, crypto's institutional infrastructure is now 12-18 months more advanced than consensus. The widest sentiment-vs-accumulation divergence since COVID.

### 6. The AI Capex Cycle Survived the War
The month's bookends: NVIDIA disappointed on Feb 27 (stock -5.5% on a modest beat) and Micron posted a historic beat on Mar 19 (stock fell anyway). In between: GTC, GPT-5.4 launch, Marvell earnings, $2.5T in projected 2026 AI spending. The hyperscalers pledged $650B+ in capex. AI infrastructure has become a national security imperative — the spending persists through war because it has to. The market punishes the spending. The demand doesn't care.

# ▸ THE MONTHLY TAKE

### From Tariff Anxiety to War Economy in 28 Days

On February 23, the biggest question in markets was whether Trump's 15% tariffs would stick past the 150-day Section 122 window. NVIDIA earnings were the most anticipated event of the month. Iran talks were "showing progress." The VIX was at 19.

By March 22, the VIX was at 27. Oil had nearly doubled. Gold had crashed. The S&P was at 2026 lows. A multi-front war involved six countries. The Fed was discussing rate hikes. And the most important event of the month — Iraq declaring force majeure — was something that wouldn't have appeared on any strategist's risk matrix four weeks earlier.

**What the month taught us about regime recognition:**

Regimes don't announce themselves. They arrive through a series of events that each seem manageable in isolation but compound into something qualitatively different. Tariffs at 15% were manageable. An Iran strike was a "one-day event." Hormuz closing was "temporary." Insurance cancellation was "technical." Each was individually absorbable. Together, they created a regime change — from a tariff-anxiety market to a war-economy market — in 28 days.

The observers who adapted fastest were the ones who tracked the *system* rather than the individual events. Our second-order chain framework (mapped on Day 1) identified the cascade: Hormuz → insurance → tankers → storage → production → force majeure. Each daily brief updated which links had fired. By Week 3, the framework was fully confirmed. The observers who struggled were the ones treating each event as independent — pricing ceasefire optionality into every escalation, expecting mean reversion that wasn't coming.

**What the month taught us about thesis management:**

Three of our tracked theses were tested hard this month. The gold Reserve Ratchet ($5,000 floor) was wrong on the level but right on the mechanism. The crypto institutional-accumulation thesis was confirmed by flows contradicting sentiment. The AI capex thesis was confirmed by Micron's earnings but the stock didn't respond. The lesson: a thesis can be fundamentally correct and still lose money in the wrong macro regime. The thesis tells you what's true. The regime tells you when it gets priced.

**Forward projection — Month 2 (March 23 – April 22):**

Three things to track: (1) Does oil sustain above $110? If yes, April inflation data will be meaningfully worse than any current forecast. (2) Does the rate hike probability reach 25%? If yes, the market reprices from "soft landing" to "stagflation." (3) Does crypto's sentiment-accumulation divergence resolve? If yes, it resolves violently — the structural setup combined with 46 days of fear creates the conditions for a sharp move.

The base-rate question for Month 2: in historical analogs, how long does a market take to fully price an involuntary supply disruption of this scale? The 1973 answer was ~3 months. We're at Month 1. The data hasn't caught up yet. When it does, the repricing accelerates.

# ▸ INNER GAME — MONTHLY

This month took us from presence practices and equanimity through the most volatile macro environment in years. The through-line across 25 daily Inner Games: **the quality of your decisions depends on the quality of your mind, and the quality of your mind depends on the quality of your connections.**

Three themes emerged:

**Equanimity under uncertainty.** The Dalai Lama, Pema Chödrön, and the Buddhist tradition of holding multiple realities simultaneously — the world can be alarming AND your Sunday morning can be yours. The people who navigated this month best were the ones who didn't let the macro consume the personal.

**Systems compassion.** Paul Farmer's insight that the system — not individual failure — determines outcomes. Applied to markets: most of the month's damage was systemic (oil cascade → gold liquidation → equity repricing → rate path shift). Blaming yourself for being in the wrong position misdiagnoses a system event as a personal one.

**Connection as foundation.** Bowlby's attachment research: securely attached people think better, take better risks, recover faster. In a month where every chart was red, the people who showed up for their relationships were the ones whose nervous systems had the stability to make good decisions.

**Monthly practice:** Review your decision-making this month. Not the outcomes — the process. Were you reactive or deliberate? Did you scroll-and-worry or connect-and-think? The market will be volatile again next month. Your process is the only thing you control.
