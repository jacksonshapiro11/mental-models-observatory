## ▸ THE UPDATE

**Jensen Huang Just Picked His Running Mate**

Marvell Technology surged 33% after Nvidia's CEO called it "the next trillion-dollar company" at Computex, its largest single-day gain ever. Nvidia took a $2 billion stake three months ago. This isn't charity. Marvell designs the custom AI accelerators and optical networking chips that connect thousands of GPUs into functioning data center systems. Nvidia builds the compute nodes; Marvell builds the connective tissue that makes the nodes worth buying. Hyperscalers designing their own chips to reduce Nvidia dependence need Marvell's custom silicon expertise. Marvell projects its custom chip business crossing $10 billion by fiscal 2029. The trillion-dollar endorsement is Nvidia publicly betting that its ecosystem-partner approach defeats Intel's vertically integrated alternative. If Marvell succeeds, Nvidia's GPUs become more valuable because they sit inside better-connected systems. If Intel's networking and custom chip strategy gains traction, both lose. Huang didn't anoint a protege. He announced a war strategy.

**The Largest Bitcoin Holder Just Blinked**

Strategy's CEO confirmed the company may sell Bitcoin for the first time in four years, ending the unconditional accumulation that anchored institutional crypto's price floor. BTC fell from $73,500 to $67,468 in 48 hours, triggering cascading liquidations that pushed DeFi TVL to $78 billion, its lowest since October 2024. The ETF wrapper designed for institutional access is now functioning as the exit ramp: $1.67 billion in weekly outflows for the third consecutive negative week. Strategy holds 528,185 BTC accumulated at $33.1 billion without ever discussing selling. The precedent: when GBTC unlocked redemptions in January 2024, $18 billion exited within six months and the trust discount collapsed from 40% to zero. Strategy operates at larger scale. If a material sale occurs by Q3, the continuous-buying floor that propped BTC above $60,000 disappears. If the language is posturing for tax planning, $67,000 becomes a higher low. Either way, the holder base composition is changing faster than the price suggests.

**Trump Put a Clock on the Biggest Binary Trade in Global Macro**

Trump said a memorandum of understanding to reopen the Strait of Hormuz could come "within a week," the first time a US official has placed a specific timeline on the resolution of the biggest binary trade in global macro. Iran maintains it has suspended all communications with Washington. The contradiction is itself a negotiating signal: both sides are performing for domestic audiences while Pakistan and Turkey work back channels. Day 95 of the war. The stated timeline converts an indefinite binary into a dated option: if Trump's week passes without an MOU, markets reprice near-term resolution sharply lower and Brent's mid-$90s equilibrium breaks upward. If the MOU materializes, oil drops as tankers transit the strait within days. But the physical damage from three months of closure does not reverse with a signature. Depleted strategic reserves, rerouted shipping networks, fertilizer shortages, defense stockpile consumption. The binary resolves. The structural costs persist for quarters.

**Smartphones Didn't Compete With Babies. They Killed the Conditions for Making Them.**

Births fell first and fastest wherever high-speed mobile connectivity arrived, according to Financial Times analysis spanning dozens of countries and decades of data. France and Poland from 2009, Mexico from 2012, Nigeria from 2013. Rich or poor, religious or secular. US teen fertility collapsed 71% since 2007. The standard debate frames fertility as economic or cultural. Both treat the decision as the bottleneck. But the decision never arises because smartphones replaced the unstructured social hours where relationships begin. No idle evenings, no accidental encounters, no relationships, no fertility decision to make. The mechanism is precondition elimination: a technology removes the upstream conditions necessary for a downstream outcome without directly affecting the outcome itself. The same mechanism is operating in AI labor markets now. Cognition's AI coding agent went from generating 16% to 80% of its own company's commits after a December model inflection point. When AI automates entry-level work, it does not compete with senior expertise. It eliminates the apprenticeship conditions where expertise develops. Companies celebrating efficiency gains are drawing down a talent buffer they cannot see depleting. Where this could break: Israel maintains a 2.9 fertility rate with 95% smartphone penetration, suggesting institutions that preserve in-person contact override the effect. The mechanism is not technological determinism. It is institutional failure, which is fixable.

**Europe's Grid Is About to Lose Both Safety Nets at Once**

Doomberg identified a converging crisis with almost no attention: Qatari LNG facilities remain offline on the wrong side of a closed shipping lane while continental water levels have fallen to multi-year lows, threatening hydroelectric capacity. The ratio of dispatchable power (gas plus hydro) to intermittent power (wind plus solar) is the master variable for grid stability. Europe spent a decade expanding intermittent capacity while dispatchable capacity held steady. Both pillars of dispatchability are now simultaneously impaired. When intermittency exceeds dispatchability, periods of simultaneously low wind and solar output become grid emergencies rather than manageable events. Europe faces an elevated probability of this crisis arriving this winter. A decade of energy transition assumptions built on stable Gulf supply just collided with physical reality. When the ideological framework contradicts the physics, the framework adjusts slowly and with reluctance.

## ▸ INTERESTING THINGS

**Lab-Grown Brains Just Moved a Muscle**

Cambridge researchers created miniature brain-and-spinal-cord systems that spontaneously send electrical signals and trigger actual muscle contractions. The adult human spinal cord does not regenerate after injury. These assembloids show the failure is not permanent but context-dependent: in the right environment, the same cells that refuse to regrow in the body will regrow in a dish. The problem may not be making neurons grow. It may be recreating the conditions under which they already know how to grow.

**The 50-Year Speed Ban Was Actually a Noise Ban**

NASA's X-59 is preparing for its first supersonic flight, designed to produce a 75-decibel "sonic thump" instead of the 105-decibel boom that killed commercial supersonic aviation when Concorde retired. The FAA banned civilian supersonic flight over US land in 1973. The constraint was never speed. It was noise. Every field has a version of this: a capability that exists but is prohibited because of a side effect. The breakthrough arrives not by increasing the capability but by eliminating the side effect.

## ▸ THE MODEL

### Metis

The ancient Greeks called it metis: practical, local knowledge that accumulates through direct engagement with a specific environment over time. It is what the experienced sailor knows about a particular harbor that no chart captures, what the veteran nurse notices before any vital sign changes. When a formal system replaces a process optimized by metis, it destroys local knowledge before anyone realizes it existed. Before replacing any existing process, ask: what problems does the current system solve that nobody has explicitly identified? The workarounds are the metis.
