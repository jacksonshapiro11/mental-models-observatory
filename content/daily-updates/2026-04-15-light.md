## ▸ THE UPDATE

**The Fed Just Got a License to Hold, Not a Reason to Cut**

March PPI printed 4.0% year over year, the highest since February 2023, but the real number was core at +0.1% monthly with services stuck at 3.8% for a fourth straight month. Energy did all the work. The tape read it dovish; the Fed read is narrower. A one-channel print is a license to hold, not a signal the underlying regime softened. The tell is whether services and transportation/warehousing PPI (already +1.3% monthly) keep accelerating in April. If the hot channel has started bleeding into the cold channel, the hold becomes a hold into stagflation, not a hold with a cut queued. That distinction is worth roughly 100 basis points of policy-path repricing by Q3.

**Three Ships Quietly Transited a Closed Strait**

The Hormuz blockade is still technically active on day three, but enforcement has gaps: three ships crossed, Pakistan and Turkey are brokering a second round of US-Iran talks, and Macron's UK-France "peaceful multinational mission" framing is materially different from how the White House has described enforcement. That is the first public US-Europe strategic fracture of the operation. Marine war-risk premiums are still 5-8x overnight while the physical cargoes move; the gap between the insurance market and the shipping market is the rate-of-change indicator. If a fourth ship transits clean inside 72 hours, the premium compresses. If Iran interdicts one vessel, this becomes a two-quarter story. The administration is negotiating through intermediaries while enforcing directly, and the two channels run on different clocks.

**Big Pharma Just Made Its First Real AI Bet**

Novo Nordisk signed a multi-year, multi-hundred-million-dollar deal routing OpenAI's frontier reasoning models into target identification and molecule design. This is not an API subscription; it is the first Pharma Big-4 making AI capability a capital-allocation line item rather than a vendor contract, and GLP-1 revenue gave Novo the balance-sheet room to fund it. The 2015-2018 Insilico/Atomwise wave underdelivered because the models were narrow. The 2026 version looks different because the reasoning layer has finally caught up to the hypothesis-generation step pharma needed a decade ago. If Roche, Pfizer, or Merck announces a comparable structural partnership by Q3, AI-pharma becomes a sector reprice.

**Claude Wrote 6.1% of Public GitHub Last Week. The Chasm Is Operational.**

Claude Code's share of public commits jumped from 3.9% in January to 6.1% in the first week of April, a 56% rise on a non-linear curve. Karpathy's framing, amplified through Zvi's Mythos writeup, is that the gap between heavy frontier users and non-users is "only growing." Income data lags capability data by two to four quarters; the first tell will be salary-band compression at the median software engineer role inside hyperscalers. The forward prediction worth tracking: by Q4 2026, at least one Fortune 500 announces a 20%+ engineering headcount reduction citing AI authoring, not attrition. When that lands, developer-tools equities reprice on unit economics, not TAM.

**Stablecoins Just Matched Visa**

On-chain stablecoin payment volume hit $350-550B in Q1 2026, matching or exceeding Visa's Q1 B2B cross-border flow. The composition is the signal: the bulk is USDC and USDT settling B2B corridors the correspondent-banking system underprices (LatAm, sub-Saharan Africa, Gulf-to-South Asia). GENIUS Act rules tighten through Q2 and force the next leg inside regulated perimeters, which reshapes Circle and the Tether-parent entities into payment infrastructure companies. The tell is what Visa and Mastercard do next. If neither has acquired a stablecoin issuer or launched a native rail by Q3, the incumbents are choosing to cede the highest-margin segment of their business.

## ▸ INTERESTING THINGS

**The Bottleneck Every AI-to-Nuclear Deal Is Quietly Assuming Away**

Every hyperscaler PPA and SMR commitment signed this year assumes US reactors refuel on schedule. The separative work units that turn mined uranium into reactor fuel were about 40% Russian before 2024 and the industry is still drawing down pre-war inventory. Centrus runs one cascade, Urenco and Orano have fixed output, and the next capacity additions land 2028-2030. If one utility delays a Q3 refuel citing fuel availability, every AI-power timeline priced for 2028 quietly slides to 2032.

**The Oldest Spiral Galaxy Just Pushed the Timeline Back 300 Million Years**

Astronomers dated an organized spiral galaxy to 12.8 billion years ago, roughly 900 million years after the Big Bang. That is 300 million years earlier than any prior model had allowed for structured galactic formation. Either early-universe dark matter was denser than assumed or gas-cooling rates were faster, or both. Observational astronomy has been ahead of simulation cosmology on this axis for three years running, and every new early-galaxy observation forces recalibration of the simulations, not the other way around.

## ▸ THE MODEL

### Institutional Path Dependence

Early choices constrain later options so thoroughly that the original rationale disappearing does not unlock the system. QWERTY is still universal a century after the mechanical reason for it was engineered away, because the coordination cost of switching rose non-linearly while everyone was typing. Use it when you are evaluating any institution's capacity to handle a new challenge: do not ask how hard the challenge is; ask how much of the institution's capacity was built for a previous challenge that no longer exists. You see it live this week in the cabinet firing cycle, where the firings are not the primary event. The depleted replacement pool is.
