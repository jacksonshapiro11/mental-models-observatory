# ▸ THE ANNUAL TAKE

### The 2026 Narrative Arc — What the Year Meant

*[To be written at year-end. This section will be 2-3x the length of a daily Take — the deepest synthesis of the year, identifying the 1-2 structural changes that will still matter in 2027 and beyond. Seeded framework below.]*

**The framework for the annual Take:**

Every year has a narrative the market tells itself in real time — and a narrative that only becomes visible in hindsight. The annual Take's job is to identify the gap between these two narratives and explain what it means for the next 12-24 months.

**Q1's contribution to the annual narrative:**
The real-time narrative in Q1 was "war shock" — a sudden, dramatic disruption that the market expected to resolve. The hindsight narrative (emerging by Q1-end) was "regime change" — a structural shift from a tariff-anxiety market to a war economy with self-reinforcing feedback loops and no visible resolution mechanism.

The annual Take will assess: which narrative was right? Did the war end and the old regime reassert itself? Or did Q1 mark the beginning of a durably different macro environment — one where commodity supply, not monetary policy, is the dominant variable?

**Key questions the annual Take will answer:**
1. What did 2026 change permanently? (Not just the events — the structural changes that persist.)
2. What did the market get wrong for the longest? (The most expensive miscalibration.)
3. What was the best framework for navigating 2026? (Which mental model produced the most actionable insight?)
4. What does 2026 mean for 2027? (The forward-looking setup.)
