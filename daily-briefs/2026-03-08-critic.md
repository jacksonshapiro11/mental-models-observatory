## WHAT I WANTED INSTEAD

**For The Model (Skippable → should be Earns space):**
A model from a domain that doesn't collide with Discovery. Options with relevance to today's brief: *Hysteresis* (physics — why systems don't return to their original state after force is removed; explains why oil insurance cancellation doesn't reverse when the war ends — directly validates the Economic Denial Architecture). Or *Phase Transitions* (physics — how small changes near critical thresholds produce discontinuous state changes; applies to the S&P death cross, the rate market pivot, the crypto Fear & Greed extreme). Either of these would add a conceptual frame without duplicating Discovery.

**For Discovery (Neutral → should be Essential):**
Replace Red Queen with something from physics or ecology. Best option: **The Yellowstone Wolf Effect** — when wolves were reintroduced to Yellowstone, they didn't just reduce elk populations, they changed where elk grazed, which allowed riverbanks to revegetate, which changed river hydrology, which changed the physical landscape of the park. One predator's presence restructured the entire ecosystem through fear alone. Apply this to Iran: the war's second-order effects (insurance cancellation, rerouting, shipping premium) are restructuring global trade geography more than the direct military action. The fear of the wolf is reshaping the river. That's Discovery-level insight — cross-disciplinary, Science Magazine-worthy, genuinely illuminating without financial context.

**For the 401(k) bullet in Crypto (thin → cut or sharpen):**
Either cut it, or make it: *"6% of Americans taking 401(k) hardship withdrawals. Historically, this rate has peaked near major crypto bottoms — 2020 COVID (5.8%), 2022 FTX (6.1%), now 6.0%. This isn't causation. It's the same underlying stress signal: when consumers are tapped out, they sell everything liquid, including crypto. The data point is a sentiment thermometer, not a cause."* That's actually editorial.

**For Deep Read URLs:**
Capture specific episode links at scan time. The SemiAnalysis link (newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/cpus-are-back-the-datacenter-cpu) is the standard. Apply it to every recommendation. "bloomberg.com/oddlots" is not.
